Hello! My name is Alexandra Gutierrez. I am currently a first-generation college student at UC Riverside. At the moment, I am a second year Japanese Lang/Lit major.

I have not had the chance to acquire a job involving Japanese, but I have had work experience in three different fields. My first "job" experience was being a TA for my high school librarian. My responsibilities called for alphabetizing, sorting, scanning books, lifting, an giving recommendations. This position lasted a whole school year as it was part of my schedule. The next was at Child and Family Services in San Bernardino. My daily tasks consisted of filing paperwork, organizing documents, talking to/caring for children who needed supervision, and answering calls. I acquired the job through an internship program at my high school. I worked at the facility for about 2 months before it was cut short by COVID-19. Although I did not work here for that long, I learned a lot about the Child Protection/Welfare Systems and its workers. My second job was at a restaurant called Sundowners. I worked here for about 6 months. The job description consisted of customer service (taking online/to-go orders), accurately working a register, and ultimately working in a fast-speed environment. In addition to the skills used for those jobs, I possess others that are just as useful, such as my Japanese. At-present I have taken up to Japanese 3, which allows me to have a casual conversation with a Japanese speaker. I have memorized about 200 essential phrases and words, including how to correctly write in hiragana, katakana, and some kanji. My listening comprehension and writing abilities are constantly improving with time and practice.

Continuing with my skills, I am known by employers and friends to be very persistent when it comes to getting work done without distractions. My independence allows me to understand my priorities and take responsibility for them when needed. With these job experiences, my learned skills in Japanese, and my natural abilities, I am confident in my ability to do well in any work environment.
